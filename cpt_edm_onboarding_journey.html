Key Observations — Strategic patterns across the funnel
01
One funnel, not two parallel tracks
Because every signup auto-creates a live MT4 account (Classic, default leverage), "live vs. demo" isn't a fork at signup. Demo is an optional, client-initiated branch from the Portal that runs alongside the core funnel, not a separate acquisition path.
02
KYC nudges branch on status, not just time
E3–E5 fire only while KYC is not started. The moment it's submitted, that branch stops and E6 (pending review) takes over — avoiding the "nudge lands mid-review" problem flagged earlier. Rejection handling sits entirely with CS.
03
Approval and the bonus hook are one moment, not two
KYC approval no longer gets its own standalone "congratulations" email. It's merged directly into Stage 3's first send, so the client isn't messaged twice in immediate succession — and the bonus becomes the concrete "why fund now" reason the moment full access unlocks.
04
No FTT re-activation — it isn't needed
Most clients who fund an account go on to trade without any nudge. Rather than build a re-activation branch for an edge case, Stage 4 stays lean: confirm the deposit, then let the client trade on their own timeline. Keeps the funnel shorter and avoids messaging people who don't need it.
05
The deposit bonus is a claim, not a credit
30% up to $500 is not automatic — the client applies in Portal > Campaign, and it credits against their first deposit. All three Stage 3 emails spell out the 4-step claim path so it's never assumed.
06
Exit copy has to hold regardless of path length
Day 14 is a fixed calendar trigger for every client, fast or slow movers alike. The handoff email can't narrate "the past two weeks" as a step-by-step recap — a client who finished KYC and funding on Day 1 would find that framing inaccurate.

04 Scope Notes & Exclusions What this journey deliberately does not cover, and why Click to expand ▾
01
Pre-registration OTP
The email verification code sent before registration completes is a spam-prevention check, not a journey step — it's skipped entirely for social sign-ins (e.g. Google) and doesn't count toward this flow.
02
KYC rejection handling
Rejected KYC submissions are handled directly by the Customer Support team, outside of this automated flow. No rejection-specific email exists in this journey.
03
Demo account branch
Demo is optional and client-initiated from the Portal — since a live account already exists by default, demo is practice, not a prerequisite. Tracked as a separate branch, out of scope for this document.
04
Account type / leverage / platform changes
Clients cannot self-serve a change from the default MT4 Classic account. Any request (MT5, different leverage, different account type) is routed to Customer Support directly — not surfaced as a CTA anywhere in this journey.
